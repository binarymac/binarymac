The Creative Method

Inspired mostly by the Scientific Method, the Creative Method is a set of techniques that I (sort of) developed and use to... well, create stuff. It can be applied to just about any discipline, from programming to painting. consists of 5 stages: Incubation, Analyzation, Experimentation, Implementation, and Documentation. Like the Scientific Method, these aren't a strict series of rules and steps, but rather a set of principles that can be loosely applied, and at any point, one may go backwards, or jump ahead.

Incubation

The first step is the inncubation of ideas and knowledge. Gather as much information as possible and use that knowledge to generate questions or ideas, even if they are "dumb". Draw inspiration from all possible sources, such as the internet, nature, or other humans. Never be afraid of being wrong or "dumb", and always approach things with and open mind.

Analyzation

After incubation, there will be a set of ideas. Expand further on some of those ideas, and analyze them. Even try combinging them. Mix and match. Study them in-depth, and grow the idea into something bigger. Layout a plan to experiment with it.

Experimentation

Execute the planned experiment. Try, fail, repeat. Go back, rethink, and learn. Reiterate through the previous stages, continuously adjusting. Experiment over and over again. Continue to experiment and gain confidence as you go along, and when you do get confident...

Implementation

Implement it. Apply what you have learned in the "real world", or a "real project". As we don't live in a perfect world, it will never be perfect, but dispite that, continue to improve by going back and applying the previous step. Never stop improving.

Documentation

Document your experiences, reflect on them, and share what you've learned. Hoarding what you know does not give you power, sharing it does. Teach others what you know the same way others taught you. A wise person that refuses to teach is not a wise person.

Continuous Creation

Repeat. The Creative Method never stops.
